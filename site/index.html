Why Intermezzo

Your body keeps the rhythm you forget to.

We've all been there — hours deep in a working session, shoulders creeping up to your ears, back slowly curling into a question mark. By the time you notice, your neck is stiff and your hips are screaming.

An intermezzo is that pause for your workday: a brief, deliberate rest that lets you set down the tension and come back a little lighter. Each break is tailored to the time of day and what your body actually needs — not just another reminder to "take a break."

What it does

Small, considered, and quietly intelligent.

Every part of Intermezzo is built to feel like a moment apart — not another notification demanding your attention.

Smart break selection

Picks 1–3 exercises per break, mixing different body areas and adapting to the time of day. Mornings ease you in; afternoons target the tension that's built up.

50+ stretches & eye-rest

Neck, shoulders, spine, chest, hips, legs, wrists — plus eye-rest breaks for screen strain. Each one written like a friend is coaching you through it.

Built-in timer

Per-side countdowns for stretches that need them. Just hit Start and follow along — no counting, no guessing how long to hold.

Videos & illustrations

Every stretch has an illustrated stick-figure guide, and many link to short video tutorials so you can see exactly what to do.

Snooze & flexible intervals

Mid-task? Snooze and pick it back up when you're ready. Set reminders anywhere from every 15 minutes to every 2 hours — you're in control.

A warm cello chime

A soft, bowed cello swell marks the start and finish — not a notification beep. The whole break is designed to feel like a moment apart.

For your mind

Care for your mind, not just your body.

Some breaks end with an optional, on-device moment for your mind — 25 short practices grounded in cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), one of the most evidence-backed approaches for easing a busy, anxious mind. Set them to Off, Occasional, or Always.

  • Grounding & breathing to settle a racing mind
  • Savoring & self-compassion — noticing the good, easing the harsh inner voice
  • Gentle reframes for the thinking traps a tired mind falls into
  • A one-tap mood check-in — kept entirely on your device

And on a string of rough days, a quiet, dismissible note points toward real human support — never an alarm, never anything sent anywhere.

How it works

Four small steps to a lighter day.

Set your rhythm

Toggle reminders on, choose your interval (15 min to 2 hours), how many exercises per break, and whether to include mind moments.

A nudge that finds you

A gentle notification and soft chime reach you in any app. Click to begin — or snooze and pick it up in a few minutes.

Follow along

Each exercise has a description, a visual guide, and a countdown. Navigate, skip, or flow through the whole routine.

Come back lighter

A little confetti, a kind word, and your streak ticks up — and, if you like, a note to your channel so friends move too.

Questions

In case you were wondering.

How do I install it? +
Intermezzo is a Chrome extension that's launching on the Chrome Web Store soon. Once it's live, you'll add it to Chrome and a quarter-rest icon appears in your toolbar — click it to set your interval, choose your break, or start one right away. No account, no setup.
Will it interrupt me when I'm focused? +
Only as often as you ask it to. You choose the interval — anywhere from 15 minutes to 2 hours — and you can toggle reminders off entirely. When a nudge does arrive, it's a soft notification you can act on or snooze for a few minutes; never a jarring pop-up.
What are mind moments? +
An optional moment for your mind that some breaks end with — 25 short practices drawn from cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT): grounding and breathing, savoring and self-compassion, gentle reframes for common thinking traps, and a one-tap mood check-in. You can set them to Off, Occasional, or Always. Everything stays on your device.
Does it work when I'm not in Chrome? +
Yes. Intermezzo sends a system notification and a chime that reach you even when Chrome is in the background — so a break finds you whether you're in another app, a fullscreen video, or a game.
Can my team or friends do this together? +
If you'd like. Paste a Discord or Slack webhook URL and your name in the popup, and Intermezzo posts a friendly note to that channel when you finish a break. Leave it blank and nothing is ever sent — it's entirely opt-in.
Is it private? And is it free? +
Yes to both. Intermezzo is free and open source, with no account and no tracking. Your settings, streak, and mood notes stay on your device — the only thing ever sent anywhere is the optional stretch-together message, to the webhook you enter yourself.
